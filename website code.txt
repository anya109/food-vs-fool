        <h2>Why Fast Food Prevention?</h2>
        <p>
            Avoiding the intake of junk food is essential for helping long-term health and overall welfare. Junk food is typically full of unhealthy refined sugars, sodium, and fats, which can knowingly increase the harm of developing chronic conditions such as obesity, heart disease, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes. These foods are often calorie-dense but lack essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and fiber, leaving our bodies undernourished despite consuming large quantities. In addition to their negative impact on physical health, fast food can also contribute to poor mental well-being, as excessive consumption has been linked to mood swings and decreased cognitive function. By reducing our intake of fast food and opting for more nutritious, balanced meals, we can enhance our energy levels, maintain a healthy weight, and reduce the likelihood of serious health problems, ultimately leading to a more vibrant and fulfilling life.

            <h3>Junk Food: The Delicious Path to Destruction</h3>
            <p>
               Imagine this: You’re hungry, in a rush, and that cheesy burger or crispy bag of chips seems like the perfect fix. It’s quick and tasty; let’s be honest, it’s kind of addictive. But have you ever stopped to think about what junk food is really doing to your body? Probably not! I’m Grishma, and in this article, I will help you understand this slow poison disguised as a treat.
            </p>
            <p>
               Junk food often has high levels of sugar, salt, unhealthy fats, and low nutritional value. It has become a big part of many people's daily diets. While it may seem convenient and tasty, regularly eating such foods can have serious consequences, affecting our overall health in many ways.                 
            </p>
            <p>
               The most noticeable and troubling effect of junk food is its role in obesity. High-calorie content, coming mainly from unhealthy fats and sugar, can lead to weight gain over time. Obesity is linked to a range of serious health issues, such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and high blood pressure. The more junk food we eat, the greater the risk of developing these chronic illnesses, which can harm our quality of life and lead to early death.
            </p>
            <p>
               Another major concern is how junk food affects mental health. Studies show that diets high in processed foods can raise the risk of depression and anxiety. The blood sugar spikes caused by sugary snacks and fast food can disrupt brain function, leading to mood swings and irritability. The lack of essential nutrients like vitamins and minerals from whole foods worsens the problem, leaving the brain vulnerable to stress and mental health challenges.
            </p>
            <p>
                Additionally, junk food is often loaded with trans fats, which are unhealthy fats found in processed foods like fried snacks and baked goods. These fats can harm heart health. Eating too much salt can lead to high blood pressure, a major risk factor for heart disease and stroke. Trans fats raise "bad" cholesterol (LDL) while lowering "good" cholesterol (HDL), leading to narrower arteries and a higher risk of heart attack.
            </p>
            <p>
               In conclusion, while junk food may provide short-term pleasure, its long-term effects on our health can be devastating. From obesity to heart disease, diabetes, and mental health problems, the dangers of consuming junk food are clear and serious. It's important to recognize these risks and take steps toward a healthier lifestyle, focusing on balanced diets that prioritize whole, nutritious foods. Your body deserves better, so make smarter food choices today to ensure a healthier tomorrow.
            </p>

            <h3>Making the Right Choices</h3>
            <p>
                Today this world is burdened with fast food and ‘ready to eat’ snacks; it’s tremendously easy to choose for ‘junk’ food when you had a stressed day and don’t want to prepare healthy meal. Moreover, these treated foods often come with high levels of sugar, and empty calories, and unhealthy fats. With time, consistent intake of fast food can result in  weight gain, heart disease, and other serious health issues. Happily, there are amply of flavorsome, healthy changes that can satisfy your body without compromising your health.
            </p>
            <p>
                When you're hunger and feel to have something crispy, avoid the chips and try to have nutritious vegetables such as cucumbers, or carrots. These are filled with lots of fiber and vitamins this will be a healthier choice! If you're in the frame of mind for something sweet, try changing candy and chocolates with a few nuts. This will give you a satisfactory crisp together with healthy fats, protein, and fiber.
            </p>
            <p>
                Greek yoghurt is an excellent substitute for ice cream for people who have a sweet tooth. It's lower in sugar, higher in protein, and creamy. To improve the taste, add some fresh berries or a honey drizzle. Another great alternative to normal fries is baked sweet potato fries. Sweet potatoes are a healthy, filling alternative to fried potatoes since they are high in fibre and vitamins.
            </p>
            <p>
                Choose a smoothie made with fresh fruits, spinach, and a base like Greek yoghurt or almond milk instead of sugary sodas if you're craving something bubbly. Smoothies are a more healthier alternative that also keeps you hydrated because they contain vitamins, minerals, and fibre. 
            </p>
            <p>
                Using air-popped popcorn instead of store-bought microwave popcorn, which is frequently laden with chemical flavourings and bad fats, is another wise move. A great snack when you're seeking something light and crispy, air-popped popcorn is low in calories, high in fibre, and free of preservatives.
            </p>
            <p>
                Small, conscious adjustments like selecting whole meals and healthier snacks can help you enjoy tasty substitutes that satisfy your cravings and fuel your body without the harmful effects of junk food.
            </p>

            <h3> ADVERSE EFFECTS OF JUNK FOOD ON MENTAL HEALTH</h3>
            <p>
               The relationship between nutrition and mental health has drawn a lot of attention lately. Fewer people are aware of the significant consequences junk food may have on mental health, even if many are aware of how nutrition affects physical health. Junk food has become a mainstay of modern diets, especially in fast-paced lifestyles, and is usually defined by its excessive levels of sugar, salt, harmful fats, and artificial additives. Nevertheless, frequent use of certain foods may have detrimental effects on mental health, impacting everything from mood to memory.
            </p>
            <p>
               The Connection Between Mental Health and Junk Food: Mental Health Issues and Depression: Junk food's link to mood disorders, such as anxiety and depression, is one of its most important effects on mental health. It has been demonstrated that diets heavy in processed foods can lead to an imbalance in brain chemicals, which are essential for mood regulation. 
            </p>
            <p>
             High Stress: The body's reaction to stress can also be impacted by junk food. Excessive consumption of junk food results in blood sugar spikes and sharp drops, which can induce tension, irritation, and exhaustion which makes it harder for them to deal with day-to-day difficulties.   
            </p>
            <p>
               In Addition, Deep sleep quality decreases when a diet heavy in sweets and saturated fats is consumed. These results highlight the critical role nutrition plays in general health and sleep quality, adding to the body of research already relating diet to sleep patterns.
            </p>
            <p>
              It is impossible to exaggerate the negative effects of junk food on mental health. Adopting a healthier, balanced diet can be an effective tool in enhancing mental well-being, as the advantages of proper nutrition for mental health become more well recognized. People can promote both their physical and mental well-being by choosing educated food choices, opening the door to a more balanced and satisfying existence. 
            </p>

            <h3>The Societal Effects of UPFs(ultra-processed foods)</h3>
            <p>
              Recently, our global culture has been gaining more popularity. Ranging from technology to trade, many fields have been impacted. One of these areas is often overlooked - food. As crucial as it is, food has been dramatically altered globally. Large food conglomerates have advertised relentlessly - to the point where “junk” food has become omnipresent in our lives. Fast food companies have opened stores around the world, making unhealthy food the go-to for many. Though this food may seem delicious, junk food actually has a number of detrimental effects on our society, global and national. 
            </p>
            <p>
             To begin with, unhealthy, ultra-processed foods pose many health risks for our society. As a whole, many diseases such as obesity, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, as well as mental health damage occur due to increased consumption of junk food. Many developed nations have large obese populations - even in children, shortening lifespans. This greatly impacts public health, creating a less healthy population. Furthermore, these harmful foods create a dent in the economy as well. Workers feel less productive and more lazy, with estimates that undernutrition and obesity cost 5% of global GDP. Moreover, increased hospital trips, medicines, and weight loss drugs lead to monetary loss as well. Many companies make a profit selling drugs and therapies, rather than addressing the issue at its roots.  
            </p>
            <p>
              Likewise, ultra-processed food also poses a great risk to cultures and local traditions. Due to absurdly high amounts of money spent, many local cuisines have to compete. Many people gain a preference for junk food as well, and neglect the cuisine of their heritage. For example, many urban citizens of Rajasthan often prefer to consume fast food due to convenience rather than dishes such as Ker Sangri or Kachri Ki Sabzi. Social isolation and loneliness, moreover, occur because of lower self-esteem and obesity. Equally important are the inequalities perpetuated by unhealthy foods. In lower-income communities, healthful foods are often less affordable in comparison to junk foods. This leads to higher consumption and more health problems for lower-income communities, often historically marginalized. In Pacific Island nations, colonialism, small populations, and genetic factors have all led to the world’s highest obesity rates due to the main food sources being pre-packaged and ultra-processed, showing the deleterious effects on developing nations.
            <p>
               All in all, UPFs (ultra-processed foods) have an excess of detriments for our society. Varying from lower life expectancies and economic productivity to loneliness, loss of heritage and inequality, junk food obviously does more harm than good. Although our global culture has been responsible for the proliferation of UPFs, our societies can learn to avoid these harmful foods and instead use our global culture for good. Curious chefs and connoisseurs can learn about healthy, traditional dishes from around the world, thus creating a happier, healthier global society.
            </p>

<script>
 
    const POLL_STORAGE_KEY = 'fastFoodPollVotes';

    
    let pollVotes = JSON.parse(localStorage.getItem(POLL_STORAGE_KEY)) || { yes: 0, no: 0 };

    const pollForm = document.getElementById('pollForm');
    const pollResultDiv = document.getElementById('pollResult');

    function renderResults() {
        const totalVotes = pollVotes.yes + pollVotes.no;
        if (totalVotes === 0) {
            pollResultDiv.textContent = "No votes yet. Be the first to vote!";
            return;
        }
        const yesPercent = ((pollVotes.yes / totalVotes) * 100).toFixed(1);
        const noPercent = ((pollVotes.no / totalVotes) * 100).toFixed(1);

        pollResultDiv.innerHTML = `
            Results:<br/>
            Yes: ${pollVotes.yes} vote(s) (${yesPercent}%)<br/>
            No: ${pollVotes.no} vote(s) (${noPercent}%)
        `;
    }

    function checkVoted() {
        return localStorage.getItem('hasVotedFastFoodPoll') === 'true';
    }

    function disableVoting() {
        const inputs = pollForm.querySelectorAll('input[name="fastFood"]');
        inputs.forEach(input => input.disabled = true);
        pollForm.querySelector('button.vote-btn').disabled = true;
    }

    
    window.onload = () => {
        renderResults();
        if (checkVoted()) {
            disableVoting();
            pollResultDiv.textContent += " Thank you for voting!";
        }
    };

    pollForm.addEventListener('submit', (e) => {
        e.preventDefault();
        if (checkVoted()) {
            alert("You have already voted. Thank you!");
            return;
        }
        const selectedOption = pollForm.fastFood.value;
        if (!selectedOption) {
            alert("Please select an option before voting.");
            return;
        }
        pollVotes[selectedOption]++;
        localStorage.setItem(POLL_STORAGE_KEY, JSON.stringify(pollVotes));
        localStorage.setItem('hasVotedFastFoodPoll', 'true');
        disableVoting();
        renderResults();
        pollResultDiv.textContent += " Thank you for voting!";
    });
</script>
